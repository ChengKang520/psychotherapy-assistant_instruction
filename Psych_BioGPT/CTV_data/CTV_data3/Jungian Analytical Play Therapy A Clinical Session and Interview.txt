NARRATOR In these clinical series, we're allowed the privilege of seeing an initial play therapy session and experiencing the interaction between therapist and child. We recognize and applaud the courage of these play therapists in allowing us to videotape their spontaneous interactions with the child. In addition, viewers are afforded the opportunity to hear from each of the therapists as they share their impressions. These are not planned experiences. There is no script. They are spontaneous interactions that allow us a unique view into each therapist's typical approach to a child in play therapy. These play therapists were eager to share their experiences in hopes that you will learn about play therapy, children, and yourself. In this video you will see Doctor John Allan, a pioneer in Jungian analytical play therapy. Doctor Allan has long been recognized as one of the leaders in the conceptualization and development of the field of play therapy. He is professor emeritus of counseling psychology at the University of British Columbia, Canada, a senior training analyst with the Pacific Northwest Society of Jungian analysts and past board member of the Association for Play Therapy. Doctor Allan is a prolific writer and includes in his impressive bibliography of over 60 articles and 16 books such notable works as, "Inscapes of a Child's World," "Jungian Counseling in Schools and Clinics," and "Written Past to Healing," "Education and Jungian Child Counseling," with co-author Judy Bitraya. According to Doctor Allan, the goal of Jungian analytical play therapy is to activate the child's individualization process and to help the child develop his or her unique identity. Through this process, children can overcome or come to terms with loss and stressful events while accepting and adapting to the demands of life. Doctor Allan further explains how he works from an attachment model, involving the purposeful use of proximity to the child.
